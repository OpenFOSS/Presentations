DO WE HAVE DYSFUNCTIONS?

  • Output from Team members was imbalanced.
    • Presentation preparation
    • Going through The 5 Dysfunctions of a Team book by Lecioni.

  • Inattention to results: Team members do not buy in to all the decisions and end up leading to lack of a clear path of action.
    • Individuals try to succeed by putting individual needs ahead of collective team goals.
    • Fear of failing the course.

Gideon Nyamachere

ACTIONS TAKEN

  • Team Building:
    • Carried out one team building exercise
      • Lesson Learned: Quiet place where we could chat in a relaxed manner.
      • Focused on keeping conversation away from project work.
      • Got to know each other better e.g. personal drives and aspirations -> Trust

  • Questionnaire to determine if team is functional.
    • Each of the 5 dysfunctions are interrelated.
      • Being susceptible to one is potentially detrimental to the team’ success.
    • Lack of Commitment
    • Avoidance of Accountability

OPPORTUNITIES FOR IMPROVEMENT

  • Pair Programming
    • Getting all the team comfortable with the process.
    • Getting time that works off class.
  • Identifying a common module to work on outside of the core.
  • Accountability: Willingness to correct others
    • Summarize your tasks at the end of meetings: everyone’s understanding is different.
  • Explicit deadlines: No ambiguity in the completeness of tasks.

Jonathan Grant

WHAT WORKED WELL

  • Creating an agenda at the beginning of every meeting.
    • Discussing the items on the agenda during the meeting.

  • Team members were passionate about the items on the table and willing to be vulnerable.
    • No guarded comments.
    • Everybody willing to admit their mistakes and weaknesses.
    • Created a trusting team: honesty.
